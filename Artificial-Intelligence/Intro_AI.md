### History of Artificial Intelligence 

| Year | Era | Milestone | Details |
|------|-----|-----------|---------|
| 1943 | Foundations | McCulloch & Pitts neuron | First mathematical model of a neuron — the conceptual seed of neural networks. |
| 1950 | Foundations | Turing Test proposed | Alan Turing's paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" posed the question "Can machines think?" |
| 1956 | Classic AI | Dartmouth Conference | John McCarthy coins the term "Artificial Intelligence." Widely considered the birth of AI as a field. |
| 1957 | Classic AI | Perceptron invented | Frank Rosenblatt's Perceptron — first trainable neural network hardware. |
| 1966 | Classic AI | ELIZA chatbot | Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT creates ELIZA, the first conversational program mimicking a therapist. |
| 1969 | Classic AI | Shakey the robot | Stanford Research Institute's Shakey — first general-purpose mobile robot able to reason about its actions. |
| 1974–80 | Classic AI | First AI Winter | Funding cuts after overpromising; Lighthill Report triggers major setback in AI research. |
| 1980 | Expert Systems | XCON expert system | Digital Equipment Corporation's XCON saves ~$40M/year configuring computer orders — AI goes commercial. |
| 1986 | Neural Era | Backpropagation popularised | Rumelhart, Hinton & Williams publish efficient backprop training for multi-layer networks. |
| 1987–93 | Expert Systems | Second AI Winter | Expert system market collapses; Lisp machines become obsolete; another funding freeze. |
| 1997 | Neural Era | Deep Blue beats Kasparov | IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov — milestone in game-playing AI. |
| 1997 | Neural Era | LSTM invented | Hochreiter & Schmidhuber introduce Long Short-Term Memory networks, solving the vanishing gradient problem. |
| 2002 | Neural Era | Roomba launched | iRobot releases Roomba — AI-driven consumer robotics enters everyday homes. |
| 2006 | Deep Learning | Deep learning revival | Geoffrey Hinton publishes deep belief networks, igniting the modern deep learning era. |
| 2011 | Deep Learning | IBM Watson wins Jeopardy! | Watson defeats Jeopardy! champions Ken Jennings and Brad Rutter using NLP and knowledge retrieval. |
| 2011 | Deep Learning | Apple Siri launched | First mainstream AI voice assistant released on iPhone 4S. |
| 2012 | Deep Learning | AlexNet & ImageNet | Krizhevsky, Sutskever & Hinton win ImageNet by a huge margin using deep CNNs — computer vision revolution begins. |
| 2014 | Deep Learning | GANs invented | Ian Goodfellow introduces Generative Adversarial Networks — foundation of image synthesis and deepfakes. |
| 2016 | Deep Learning | AlphaGo beats Lee Sedol | DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats world Go champion 4–1 — a game considered too complex for brute-force AI. |
| 2017 | Modern AI | Transformer architecture | Vaswani et al. publish "Attention Is All You Need" — Transformers become the backbone of all modern LLMs. |
| 2018 | Modern AI | BERT by Google | Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers — revolutionizes NLP benchmarks and search. |
| 2019 | Modern AI | GPT-2 by OpenAI | 1.5B parameter language model; OpenAI initially withholds it citing misuse concerns. |
| 2020 | Modern AI | GPT-3 released | 175B parameter model — demonstrates few-shot learning at scale; transforms NLP research and industry. |
| 2021 | Modern AI | DALL·E & CLIP by OpenAI | Text-to-image generation becomes viable; CLIP links language and vision representations. |
| 2022 | Generative AI | ChatGPT launched | OpenAI's ChatGPT (GPT-3.5) reaches 100M users in 2 months — fastest-growing consumer app in history. |
| 2022 | Generative AI | Stable Diffusion released | Open-source text-to-image model democratises AI image generation globally. |
| 2023 | Generative AI | GPT-4 & multimodal AI | OpenAI releases GPT-4 with image understanding; Google releases Bard; AI arms race accelerates across industry. |
| 2023 | Generative AI | Claude by Anthropic | Anthropic releases Claude, trained with Constitutional AI for safer and more reliable assistant behaviour. |
| 2023 | Generative AI | Llama open-source models | Meta releases Llama & Llama 2, enabling open-source LLM research and deployment worldwide. |
| 2024 | Generative AI | Gemini, GPT-4o, Claude 3 | Google launches Gemini Ultra; OpenAI ships GPT-4o with native voice/vision; Anthropic releases Claude 3 family (Haiku, Sonnet, Opus). |
| 2025 | Generative AI | Reasoning & agentic AI | OpenAI o1/o3, DeepSeek R1, Gemini 2.0, Claude 3.7 Sonnet push frontier reasoning; AI agents and multi-step autonomy become mainstream. |
